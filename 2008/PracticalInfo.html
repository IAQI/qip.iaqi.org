Visas

If you are not an Indian citizen, you will probably need a visa. Please apply early at an Indian consulate. Getting a visa is often time consuming.

Flights

Mr. Sanjay Goel (summit@alpcord.net, summitalpcord@gmail.com), our events manager might be able to help you find air tickets. Please write to him with your travel plans if you are interested.

Accommodation

  • Participants from India. We have reserved a limited number of guest house rooms and hostels for Indian participants. To arrange your accommodation, fill out the information on the registration server. Apply early.

    We have assigned participants accommodation based on the requests we received. Please check that your arrival and departure dates are correct: [Room assignment].

  • Participants from outside India. Participants are responsible for arranging their accommodation. Hotels in Delhi get expensive and difficult to get in December. So, please apply early.

    SUMMIT: Alpcord Network, a conference management company, is our official events manager. Through them, we have blocked some rooms at convenient hotels. They promise us that they will provide you the best rates and recommend that you apply before 1 November 2007. It is expected that this rate is significantly less than the rates the hotels offer to customers who approach them directly. If this is not the case, please let the organisers of QIP 2008 know.

    Please visit the QIP accommodation page set up by Summit-Alpcord to learn about the services they offer. There is a form there, which you can fill out and and email to Mr. Sanjay Goel (summit@alpcord.net, summitalpcord@gmail.com) with a copy to .

    At present we have rooms at the following places. To see their locations, type the addresses on Google Maps. They are a little distance away from the QIP venue. A commute of up to 15km is reasonable on the wide Delhi roads. QIP 2008 will arrange transport between these hotels and the venue. Hotels that are closer are more expensive.

    • Chintan guest house: We have heard good reports about this place from several sources (including faculty at IIT Delhi). This is a pleasant place with all the basics. The breakfast is not included in the room rate, but you can buy yourself one for Rs. 50. Despite the slightly longer commute that staying here involves, this place offers good value for money.

    • Hotel Grand Sartaj: A regular 3-star hotel. Closer to the venue. Rooms have been recently refurbished. Easy commute. Reasonable eating places close by.

    • Asian International: A regular 2-star hotel. Close to the venue, and very close to the Connaught Place, in the heart of the city, if that is where you want to be.

      As Summit-Alpcord find more places, they will add them to their web site.

      Also, FSTTCS 2007 which immediately precedes QIP 2008 uses a different company, whom you can approach as well. Please consult the FSTTCS accommodation page for more information and for tips on where to stay.

Travel

Delhi gets fog in December, and flights are often delayed.
